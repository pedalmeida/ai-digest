Personal AI knowledge should be explicit, portable files—not locked in black boxes.

01
Key signals
  • ◆ Explicit memory beats implicit: Wiki-based knowledge artifacts let you see exactly what the AI knows about you. Inspectable, not mysterious.
  • ◆ Data portability is power: Store memory as universal files (markdown, images) on your machine, not in proprietary AI systems. Use any tool—Obsidian, CLI, agents—over it.
  • ◆ BYOAI matters: Swap Claude for open-source models, fine-tune on your wiki, keep AI companies competing. You control which model runs over your data.
What this means for you

This is the architecture you need for building sticky AI products that users own. Instead of selling lock-in ("our AI learns you better"), sell agency and portability. The competitive advantage shifts to UI and agents that make file management effortless—not to hoarding user data. Also: agent proficiency is the core skill. Practice now.

Aaron Levie
CEO · Box

Context layer is now the AI stack. Models can't know all enterprise workflows—agents must search, reason, and adapt.

02
Key signals
  • ◆ Knowledge is fragmented: Every user sees different data with different permissions. No single model can hold everyone's secrets. The bottleneck is intelligence, not access—AI must process raw info faster than humans.
  • ◆ Agent design mirrors human work: Old approach (embeddings → similarity search → chunks) failed when irrelevant chunks ranked high or documents contradicted. New approach: agents search like users but expand queries, read full context, reason about misses.
  • ◆ Model improvements unlock new use-cases: Better tool-calling, larger accurate context windows, advanced reasoning, and coding ability mean agents can now handle qualitatively different problems—document synthesis, multi-doc reasoning, self-correction at scale.
What this means for you

Stop fighting model limitations with hacks. The context layer (smart search, full-doc reading, reasoning loops) is where the moat is. As you build AI products for enterprise, design for dynamic context retrieval and agent reasoning—not static embeddings. This is how Box Agent works and why it's powerful. Your product's value isn't the model; it's how agents navigate the user's actual information landscape.

AI can reverse government opacity—citizens armed with agents can see spending, voting, lobbying more clearly than before.

03
Key signals
  • ◆ Transparency bottleneck was intelligence, not data: 4000-page bills, budgets, FOIA dumps, voting records are public but unprocessable by humans. Only investigative journalists could make sense of it.
  • ◆ AI democratizes accountability: Agents can now track spending patterns, diff legislation, correlate lobbying → votes → regulation, flag regulatory capture. Applies even more to local government (less coverage, smaller populations, higher impact).
  • ◆ Two-way sword: Same tools enable surveillance and control. Worth being mindful, but optimistic overall that participation + transparency improve democracies.
What this means for you

This is a massive product opportunity: transparency-as-a-service for civic engagement. Imagine agents that summarize city council votes, track local spending, correlate zoning decisions to developer influence. The market (citizens, journalists, advocacy groups) has huge appetite but no tools. This sits at the intersection of AI capability and democratic demand.

Takeaway

Physicists moving into AI aren't pivoting—they're recognizing AI as the next frontier after particle physics hit diminishing returns on new experimental apparatus.

06
Key points
  • ◆ Physics → AI pipeline: High-energy physicists realized after the Higgs discovery that the next leverage isn't better particle accelerators—it's AI. Same skillset (principled thinking, hard science) applies with higher impact.
  • ◆ Foundation models for atoms: Periodic Labs is building AI for material science—domain-specific models that understand chemistry, not just language. Different architecture than LLMs but same scaling principles.
  • ◆ Context matters: Google Brain era (2016–2017) was small teams, few GPUs, high entropy in research. Fedus worked on sparsity and scale. Now he's applying those lessons to physical world problems—where data is scarcer, validation is harder, but impact is massive.
What this means for you

Watch Periodic Labs and domain-specific foundation models closely. They represent the next wave after general-purpose LLMs: specialized models for verticals with unique data and physics constraints. If you're building AI products for manufacturing, materials, biotech, or any "atoms" domain, the playbook is: collect domain data → build efficient foundation model → layer agents on top. Also note: physicists think differently about scaling and constraints than software engineers. Their arrival in AI is shifting how we approach real-world problems.
